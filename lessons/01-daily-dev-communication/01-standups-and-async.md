## 2. Article (excerpt)

> **Blocker Escalation**
> Blockers are the most critical aspect of async dailys. In a synchronous meeting, a blocker
> is immediately visible – async, it can easily go unnoticed. That's why a defined
> **escalation** path is essential: flag the blocker, get a response within a defined window,
> and if no solution emerges in the thread → short **huddle** or direct call. Async is the
> default, not the dogma. The engineering manager credo from practice: the biggest killer for
> async dailys is a lack of **follow-through** on blockers. When team members start feeling
> that their **blocker** reports disappear into the void, trust in the format dies – and the
> format dies with it.
>
> **Anti-Patterns**
> Copy-paste updates: identical posts on consecutive days signal **disengagement** or lack of
> reflection.
>
> **Benefits — for management**
> For management, the format **scales** effortlessly. A synchronous daily with five people
> takes 15 minutes – with fifteen people, often 45. Async updates scale linearly: more people,
> more posts, but no exponentially growing time cost.
>
> **"Nobody Reads the Updates"**
> In teams with more than eight to ten people, the read rate of updates drops dramatically.
> The channel becomes a **box-ticking** exercise that serves no one.
>
> **Loss of Team Interdependence**
> The weightiest objection comes from the agile community itself: the Scrum Guide defines the
> purpose of the Daily Scrum as inspecting progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapting the
> Sprint Backlog. This requires synchronous communication – a shared moment in which the team
> develops a plan for the next day.
>
> **Context Switching Through Permanent Thread Monitoring**
> Cal Newport argues that asynchronous communication doesn't protect focus time but undermines
> it. Open threads create a permanent pull. When you know that colleagues could respond to your
> blocker at any time, you check the channel every few minutes – and pay the price of a
> **context switch** each time.
>
> **When Async Works Poorly**
> Newly assembled teams or during **onboarding** phases, where personal contact is essential
> for trust and knowledge transfer.
>
> **The Hybrid Model**
> Model B: async-default with optional **sync**. All updates run **async**. Two to three times
> per week, there's an optional 10-minute window for a short sync call – anyone with something
> to discuss joins. Anyone without keeps working.

## 5. Discrimination Task

Выбери слово, которое подходит по смыслу лучше.

1. We need a quick (**sync** / **huddle**) to sort this out right now, face-to-face.
2. Reporting a blocker isn't enough — what actually matters is the (**escalation** / **follow-through**) afterward.
3. Every extra meeting invite is a tiny (**blocker** / **context switch**) — it's not stopping your task, just costing you focus.
4. Filling out the retro form without real reflection turns it into (**box-ticking** / **disengagement**).
5. Her first two weeks weren't just about learning the tools — it was full (**onboarding** / **training**), meeting the team, understanding how decisions get made.
6. It's not just that the teams cooperate well — payments and infra are so tightly coupled that this level of (**interdependence** / **cooperation**) means one failure blocks everyone.
7. We're not fully (**async** / **remote**) — the point isn't where people work, it's that nobody needs to be online at the same time.

6. "This checklist doesn't help anyone, people just fill it out because they have to." — *(box-ticking)*

## Bonus Vocabulary (self-flagged from the article)

Words you singled out while reading — not part of the core 12, but worth keeping.

| Word | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **thread** | a chain of connected messages/replies in a chat or forum, all responding to the same original post | *"Let's keep this in the thread, not the main channel — it's getting noisy."* |
| **to emerge** | to appear or become visible gradually, often after being unclear or hidden | *"If no solution emerges in the thread within an hour, we escalate."* |
| **into the void** *(idiom)* | disappearing with no response or trace, as if no one is there to receive it | *"Every blocker I posted felt like shouting into the void — nobody replied."* |
| **consecutive** | following one right after another, without a break | *"She's missed three consecutive standups this month."* |
| **post** *(noun)* | a single message published in a channel, thread, or feed — the noun form of "to post" | *"His posts were word-for-word identical two days in a row."* |
| **to undermine** | to gradually weaken something, often in a way that isn't obvious at first | *"Constant notifications undermine deep focus, even on an async-first team."* |
| **substance** | the real, meaningful content of something, as opposed to being empty or superficial | *"His feedback had no substance — just 'looks good to me.'"* |

Common trap: **thread** vs **threat** — different sound (/θred/ vs /θret/), easy to mix up when
reading fast.
